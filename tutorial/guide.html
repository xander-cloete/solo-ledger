What Solo Ledger is

Solo Ledger keeps a running picture of your personal finances. Everything you type is saved only on this device, inside your browser — nothing is uploaded to a server, and you never sign in. Close the tab and your data is still here when you come back.

It answers three everyday questions:

Where's my money going?

Record income and expenses each month and watch your rolling balance carry forward — last month's leftover becomes this month's starting point.

What am I worth?

Your net worth is your everyday balance plus the value of every investment, tracked over time on one chart.

Quick start

Five minutes to set up

  1. Open Settings and set your currency symbol and starting balance — your bank balance today, so the ledger lines up with reality.
  2. Add your income streams on the Income page (e.g. Salary, Freelance). The amount pre-fills each month.
  3. Add your expenses — rent, groceries, a yearly insurance bill. Solo Ledger places each one in the right month automatically.
  4. Add any investments and record their balance now and then to see real growth.
  5. Export a backup from Settings now and then, so your data is safe if you switch devices.

The home screen

Your dashboard

The Dashboard is a read-only summary — it invents nothing, it just mirrors everything you've entered elsewhere. It's the first thing you see when you open the app.

  1. Net worth is the big number at the top: your liquid balance plus the value of every investment.
  2. Liquid balance vs. Investments break that total in two — spendable money now, and money that's invested.
  3. Your progress shows a gentle level, a saving streak, and how much of this month's income you've used. (You can hide this in Customize.)
  4. Net worth over time charts your last 12 months so you can see the trend at a glance.
  5. This month spells out income, expenses, and the difference between them.

Money in

Recording income

Income is organised into streams — your recurring sources of money. Each new month pre-fills with the stream's default amount, so you only adjust what actually changed.

  1. Use the month switcher (top right) to pick which month you're recording.
  2. In Income for this month, type the real amount received for each stream — it overrides the default just for that month.
  3. To add a new source, type a name and a default in Income streams and press Add.
  4. Untick Active to retire a stream without deleting its history.
  5. The rolling balance at the top shows what carried in, plus this month's income, minus expenses, and what carries into next month.

Money out

Tracking expenses

Add an expense once and Solo Ledger works out which months it belongs to. There are three kinds, so a yearly bill doesn't have to be retyped every month.

  1. In Add an expense, give it a name and amount.
  2. Pick a type: Monthly (recurs every month — optionally for a set number of months), Yearly (lands on its due date's month each year), or One-off (a single month).
  3. Set when it starts, then press Add expense.
  4. Tick Itemise to build the total from individual items (qty × price, by store) — handy for groceries.
  5. Use Edit or ✕ in All expenses to change or remove one later.

Holdings

Tracking investments

Each investment is a portfolio you update by hand — no bank linking. The clever part: Solo Ledger separates real growth from money you simply added, so your returns aren't flattered by deposits.

  1. In Add a portfolio, enter a name, the date you started, and your initial amount (your starting capital).
  2. Now and then, press Record balance to log what it's worth today.
  3. Use Contribute or Withdraw when you add or take out money — these don't count as growth.
  4. Read the growth figures for 1 month, 3 months and 1 year, plus all-time growth beside the name.

Make it yours

Themes & settings

Solo Ledger ships with fourteen full visual "worlds". Pick whichever fits your mood — it applies instantly and is remembered on this device.

  1. On Customize, click any theme to switch the whole app. The active one is ticked.
  2. Toggle Show the progress panel off for a plain, all-business dashboard.
  3. Turn on Reduce motion to calm animations (your device's own setting is always respected too).
Back up your data. Because everything lives only in this browser, clearing your browser data or switching devices will lose it. On Settings → Backup, press Export backup to save a file, and Import backup… to restore it elsewhere. Do this regularly.
