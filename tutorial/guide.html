What Solo Ledger is

Solo Ledger keeps a running picture of your personal finances. Everything you type is saved only on this device, inside your browser — nothing is uploaded to a server, and you never sign in. Close the tab and your data is still here when you come back.

It answers three everyday questions:

Where's my money going?

Record income and expenses each month and watch your rolling balance carry forward — last month's leftover becomes this month's starting point.

What am I worth?

Your net worth is your everyday balance plus the value of every investment, tracked over time on one chart.

Quick start

Five minutes to set up

  1. Open Settings and set your currency symbol and starting balance — your bank balance today, so the ledger lines up with reality.
  2. Add your income streams on the Income page (e.g. Salary, Freelance). The amount pre-fills each month.
  3. Add your expenses — rent, groceries, a yearly insurance bill. Solo Ledger places each one in the right month automatically.
  4. Add any investments and record their balance now and then to see real growth.
  5. Export a backup from Settings now and then, so your data is safe if you switch devices.

The home screen

Your dashboard

The Dashboard is a read-only summary — it invents nothing, it just mirrors everything you've entered elsewhere. It's the first thing you see when you open the app.

  1. Net worth is the big number at the top: your liquid balance plus the value of every investment, minus what you owe.
  2. Liquid balance, Investments and Debt break that total into its parts — spendable money now, money that's invested, and what you still owe. (The Debt panel appears once you've added a liability.)
  3. Your progress shows a gentle level, a saving streak, and how much of this month's income you've used. (You can hide this in Customize.)
  4. Net worth over time charts your recent history, then continues past a “now” marker as a dashed projection — with 1, 3 and 5-year estimates in the cards beneath.
  5. This month spells out income, expenses, and the difference between them.

Money in

Recording income

Income is organised into streams — your recurring sources of money. Each new month pre-fills with the stream's default amount, so you only adjust what actually changed.

  1. Use the month switcher (top right) to pick which month you're recording.
  2. In Income for this month, type the real amount received for each stream — it overrides the default just for that month.
  3. To add a new source, type a name and a default in Income streams and press Add.
  4. Untick Active to retire a stream without deleting its history.
  5. The rolling balance at the top shows what carried in, plus this month's income, minus expenses, and what carries into next month.

Money out

Tracking expenses

Add an expense once and Solo Ledger works out which months it belongs to. There are three kinds, so a yearly bill doesn't have to be retyped every month.

  1. In Add an expense, give it a name and amount.
  2. Pick a type: Monthly (recurs every month — optionally for a set number of months), Yearly (lands on its due date's month each year), or One-off (a single month).
  3. Set when it starts, then press Add expense.
  4. Tick Itemise to build the total from individual items (qty × price, by store) — handy for groceries.
  5. In Your expenses, click Edit to amend an expense right where it sits — Save or Cancel returns it to the list. ✕ removes it.
  6. The list shows This month by default; switch to All-time to see past one-offs. Use the Show chips to hide sections you don't need and Sort to order by name, amount or date.

Holdings

Tracking investments

Each investment is a portfolio you update by hand — no bank linking. The clever part: Solo Ledger separates real growth from money you simply added, so your returns aren't flattered by deposits.

  1. In Add a portfolio, enter a name, the date you started, and your initial amount (your starting capital).
  2. Now and then, press Record balance to log what it's worth today.
  3. Use Contribute or Withdraw when you add or take out money — these don't count as growth.
  4. Read the growth figures for 1 month, 3 months and 1 year, plus all-time growth beside the name.
  5. Press Project to look ahead — see the next page.
  6. The Show chips and Sort menu (also on Income and Expenses) let you hide the cards or quick-edit list and order portfolios by name, amount or start date.

Looking ahead

Projections

Solo Ledger doesn't only record the past — it estimates where your money is heading, using your own numbers. There's no target to guess at: it reads how each portfolio has actually grown and carries that forward.

  1. On any portfolio, press Project. The panel opens with the growth rate Solo Ledger worked out from your history — measured carefully, so money you added isn't mistaken for money you earned.
  2. Not enough track record to trust it? Type your own annual growth % to override the estimate — or press “use my history instead” to switch back.
  3. Add a monthly contribution to see how a steady top-up compounds over time.
  4. Read where it could land in 1, 3, 5 and 10 years — the figures update live as you type.
  5. Your Dashboard rolls every portfolio into a single forward line on the net-worth chart, with 1, 3 and 5-year marks.
An estimate, not a promise. Markets don't move in straight lines. A projection assumes steady growth, so read it as the shape of where you're heading — a guide, not a guarantee.

What you owe

Tracking liabilities

Net worth isn't only what you own — it's what you own minus what you owe. The Liabilities page tracks your debts so the picture is honest, and lets you keep each interest rate current as the repo rate moves.

  1. Add a liability — give it a name, pick a type (home loan, vehicle, loan, credit card, overdraft), and enter the amount owed, the interest rate, and an optional monthly repayment.
  2. As you pay it down, press Update balance to record what's left — the progress bar shows how much you've cleared.
  3. When the repo rate changes, press Update rate and set the new rate and the date it took effect. Past rates are kept, so your history stays accurate.
  4. Press Project to see when a debt clears and how much interest it'll cost at your current repayment — adjust the repayment to compare.
  5. The Total owed panel sums every debt (with a blended rate), and your Dashboard subtracts it from net worth automatically.
If a repayment can't cover the interest, Solo Ledger says so — the balance won't shrink until you pay more than the monthly interest.

Make it yours

Choosing a theme

Solo Ledger ships with fourteen full visual "worlds". Pick whichever fits your mood — it applies instantly and is remembered on this device.

  1. On Customize, click any theme to switch the whole app. The active one is ticked.
  2. Toggle Show the progress panel off for a plain, all-business dashboard.
  3. Turn on Reduce motion to calm animations (your device's own setting is always respected too).

Preferences

Settings & backup

Settings is where you match the app to your own money — and, most importantly, where you keep your data safe.

  1. Set your currency symbol (shown beside every amount) and currency code, then press Save.
  2. Enter your starting balance — your real bank balance today — so the rolling ledger lines up with reality. The preview shows how it'll read.
  3. Under Reminders, choose to be nudged 3 months and/or 1 month before a yearly expense is due, so a big bill never surprises you.
  4. Optionally Enable browser notifications to be alerted by your device, not just inside the app.
  5. Use Export backup to save all your data to a file, and Import backup… to restore it on a new device.
Back up regularly. Because everything lives only in this browser, clearing your browser data or switching devices will lose it. A backup file is the only way to move or recover your ledger.
